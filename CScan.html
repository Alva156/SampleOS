What is C-Scan

A modified variation of the SCAN Disk Scheduling Algorithm, the Circular SCAN (C-SCAN) Scheduling Algorithm addresses the inefficiencies of the SCAN algorithm by providing more consistent service to the requests. Similar to the Elevator Algorithm SCAN, C-SCAN shifts the head from one end to the other, fulfilling all demands. But as soon as the head reaches the other end, it instantly goes back to the disk's beginning, service no requests along the journey, and then it begins servicing all over again at the beginning. Because it effectively interprets the cylinders as a circular list that loops around from the last cylinder to the first, this is also known as the "Circular Elevator Algorithm."

The advantages of C-Scan are it works well with moderate to heavy loads and it provides better response time and uniform waiting time. The disadvantages of C-Scan are it may not be fair to service requests for tracks at the extreme end and it has more seek movements as compared to the SCAN Algorithm.
