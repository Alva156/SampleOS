What is SRTF Scheduling

Shortest remaining time first is the preemptive version of the Shortest job first. in the Shortest Remaining Time First (SRTF) scheduling method, the process that has the least amount of time left to finish is chosen to run. Processes always continue until they finish or a new process that takes less time is added. This is because the process that is presently executing is the one that by definition has the least amount of time left, and that time should only decrease as execution moves forward. Because the overhead costs of the SRTF algorithm are not taken into account, it processes tasks quicker than the SJF method.

The advantages of SRTF are short processes are handled very fast and the system also requires very little overhead since it only makes a decision when a process completes or a new process is added. The disadvantages of SRTF are like the shortest job first, it also has the potential for process starvation and ong processes may be held off indefinitely if short processes are continually added.
